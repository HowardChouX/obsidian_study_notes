## 23-6-1（网课的优缺点）



### 原文错误修改
- `online classes is`→ ​**online classes are**​ (主谓一致)
        
    - `you will be excused by others`→ 此处“excused”用词不当，想表达“被打扰”，应为 ​**disturbed**​ 或 ​**interrupted**。
        
    - `it is good in the all`→ 中式表达，应为 ​**On the whole, it is beneficial.​**​ 或 ​**All in all, it does more good than harm.​**​
        
    
- ​**词汇与搭配错误**​：
    
    - `convient`→ ​**convenient**​ (拼写)
        
    - `learn more difficult knowledge`→ 搭配不自然，可改为 ​**acquire more advanced knowledge**。
        
    - `following teachers‘ step`→ 应为 ​**following the teacher‘s pace**​ 或 ​**the teacher‘s steps**。
        
    - `ask question everywhere`→ 表意模糊，可能是 ​**ask questions anytime and anywhere**​ (随时随地提问)。
        
    - `in the funrther what‘s more`→ 混合了“furthermore”和“what‘s more”，应为 ​**Furthermore,​**​ 或 ​**What‘s more,​**。
        
    - `at a noisy room`→ 介词误用，应为 ​**in a noisy room**。
        
    - `let us pay attention`→ “let”稍显口语化，可改为 ​**help us focus (on the lesson)​**​ 或 ​**ensure that we concentrate**。


### 修改后的范文

**Dear Sir or Madam,​**​

​**I am delighted to share my opinions on online classes based on my personal experience.​**​

​**On the one hand, online classes offer remarkable convenience.​**​ They allow us to schedule our learning freely, which enables us to acquire more advanced knowledge at our own pace rather than merely following the teacher‘s steps. Moreover, we can ask questions anytime and anywhere via digital platforms.

​**On the other hand, there are some drawbacks.​**​ Staring at the screen for extended periods can be harmful to our eyes. Furthermore, studying in a noisy environment at home can easily lead to distractions. Therefore, it is crucial for both schools and students to take measures to improve focus, such as creating quiet study spaces and incorporating more interactive elements into the lessons.

​**All in all, while online learning has its challenges, its convenience makes it a valuable mode of education. With continuous improvements, it can become even more effective.​**​

### 修改后的范文
**Should University Libraries Be Open to the Public?​**​

Dear Madam or Sir,

**I am pleased to share my view on** whether university libraries should be accessible to the public. **I firmly believe that** they should be open, **for the following reasons**.

**First**, reading is essential for personal and social development. Allowing the public to use university libraries would provide broader access to knowledge, **especially for those who lack educational resources**. **Moreover**, such openness helps build a stronger learning community. **For instance**, public users might bring diverse perspectives, while students can benefit from a more vibrant academic atmosphere. **Some may worry about** noise or disorder, but these can be addressed through practical measures **like** time restrictions for visitors, designated quiet zones, and clear usage rules, rather than simply closing the doors.

**In conclusion**, opening university libraries to the public promotes social learning and educational equality. With proper management, it can be beneficial for all.

# 翻译批改


### 23-6-1（终身教育）

#### 原文错误修改

1. ​**拼写与大小写错误**​
    
    - “In china” → 应为“In China”（专有名词需大写）。
        
    - “continue education” → 应为“continuing education”（继续教育的固定表达）。
        
    
2. ​**语法错误**​
    
    - “educating a person in his all life” → 中式英语，建议改为“lifelong education”或“education throughout one’s life”。
        
    - “a effective way” → 冠词错误，应为“an effective way”。
        
    - “to let the person get educated in their all life” → 表达冗余且语法混乱，建议简化为“to enable individuals to pursue education throughout their lives”。
        
    - “High school is regarded as a room to make the intelligent person better” →
        
        - “high school”在原文中指“高校”（应译为“colleges/universities”）；
            
        - “room”用词不当，应为“place”或“base”；
            
        - “make the intelligent person better”表述不准确，建议改为“cultivate talents”或“enhance intellectual abilities”。
            
        
    - “which has advanced teaching ways and outstanding teaching source” →
        
        - “teaching ways”宜改为“teaching methods”；
            
        - “source”应为复数“sources”。
            
        
    - “many school is suitable the demand” → 主谓不一致（schools are），且“suitable”用法错误，应改为“adapt to the demand”。
        
    - “There are planned to find a new way...” → 句式杂糅，建议改为“They have planned to explore a new way...”。
        
    - “suitable for Chinese suitcase” → 明显笔误，应为“suitable for China’s situation/national conditions”。
        
    - “It will make great influence on developing country's strategy” →
        
        - “make influence”搭配错误，应为“have a great influence”；
            
        - “developing country's strategy”有歧义，应明确为“the national development strategy”。
            
        
    
3. ​**用词与搭配不当**​
    
    - “teaching ways” → 宜用“teaching methodologies”或“educational approaches”。
        
    - “outstanding teaching source” → “source”应改为“resources”（教学资源）。
        
    - “firms” → 原文“用人单位”更贴切应为“employers”或“enterprises”。
        
    
4. ​**句子结构问题**​
    
    - 多处使用简单句堆砌，缺乏衔接词，导致逻辑松散。例如“Thus”后未形成有效因果衔接。
        
    - 中文思维直译明显，如“to let the person get educated in their all life”。
        
    
5. ​**理解核心概念**​：
    
    - 原文中“终身教育”应译为“lifelong education”；“继续教育”译为“continuing education”。
        
    - “高校”指高等院校，应译为“colleges and universities”，而非“high school”。
        
    
6. ​**避免直译，注重英语表达习惯**​：
    
    - 例如“构建终身教育体系”可译为“build a lifelong education system”；“办学主体”可译为“main provider of education”。
        
    
7. ​**基础语法巩固**​：
    
    - 需强化**冠词**​（a/an/the）、**单复数**​（school → schools, source → sources）、**主谓一致**​（school is → schools are）等基础语法点。
        
    
8. ​**使用更准确的词汇与搭配**​：
    
    - “人才培养的基地” → “a base for talent cultivation”。
        
    - “适应社会需求” → “meet social needs”或“adapt to societal demands”。
        
    - “发挥更大的作用” → “play a greater role”。
